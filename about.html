About Soko Yetu

Built for East Africa, by people who use it too.

Soko Yetu started with a simple idea: East Africa needed one place to buy and sell anything, without being limited to a single country. Whether you're in Nairobi, Kampala, Kigali, Dar es Salaam, or anywhere across the region, Soko Yetu connects buyers and sellers across borders, currencies, and categories.

What we do

Soko Yetu is a classifieds marketplace. We don't sell anything ourselves — we give people a simple, trustworthy place to list what they have and find what they need. From vehicles and property to electronics, fashion, jobs, and services, our categories cover the things people across East Africa buy and sell every day.

Where we operate

We currently support listings across nine countries: Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, Congo (DRC), Burundi, South Sudan, Somalia, and Ethiopia. Each country's listings show prices in the local currency, so buyers and sellers always know exactly what they're dealing with.

Our approach to safety

Buying and selling between strangers always carries some risk, so we built safety guidance directly into the platform — from buyer safety tips on every listing, to phone number masking until a buyer chooses to reveal it, to a straightforward way to report suspicious ads. We'd rather a transaction be slow and safe than fast and risky.

Where we are today

Soko Yetu is currently in active development. The marketplace experience you see today — browsing, searching, posting ads, managing your listings — is fully built and working. We're currently building the backend systems that will make accounts, messaging, and payments fully real and permanent. We'll be rolling these out region by region as they're ready.
