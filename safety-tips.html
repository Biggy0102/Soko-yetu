Staying safe on Soko Yetu

A few habits that keep buying and selling safe for everyone.

For buyers

🤝 Meet in a safe, public place

Choose a busy, well-lit location to meet the seller — a shopping centre, petrol station, or public market works well. Avoid meeting alone in unfamiliar or isolated areas, especially for high-value items.

🔍 Inspect before you pay

Check that the item matches the ad — condition, features, and any specs mentioned. For electronics, test that it actually powers on and works as described. Never pay before you've seen and checked the item.

💰 Avoid paying in advance

Be cautious of any seller who asks for a deposit or full payment before you've met or seen the item, especially for "delivery" arrangements. This is one of the most common scam patterns.

📦 Match the package to what you inspected

If a seller hands you a sealed box, make sure it's the exact item you checked — not swapped at the last moment. Open and confirm before completing payment.

🚩 Trust your instincts

If a deal feels rushed, the price feels unrealistically low, or the seller avoids answering direct questions, it's okay to walk away. Use the "Report this ad" option on any listing that feels off.

For sellers

📱 Use phone number masking

By default, your number is hidden until a buyer chooses to reveal it. This cuts down on spam calls and gives you more control over who reaches out.

💬 Keep early conversations on-platform

Use the chat feature for initial conversations before sharing personal details. This creates a record of the conversation in case anything goes wrong.

🏦 Be cautious with "overpayment" requests

Be wary of buyers who send more than the agreed amount and ask for the difference back, or who want to pay via unfamiliar methods. This is a known scam pattern targeting sellers.

📍 Choose your meeting location

As the seller, you can suggest the meeting place — pick somewhere you feel comfortable and that's easy for you to reach safely.

Reporting a problem

Every listing has a "Report this ad" option. If you encounter a scam, suspicious behavior, or a prohibited item, please report it — this helps keep Soko Yetu safe for everyone in the community.
